Curtain lifts on Wellington Fashion Week

Stilettos are strutting the capital as Wellington Fashion Week opens with a show by renowned Kiwi label WORLD. It's the second time the event has been held and this year almost 40 designers, including up and coming local brands, as well as the likes of Trelise Cooper, Starfish, Kathryn Wilson and Ruby are taking part.
WORLD's opening night catwalk show featured Great Gatsby-style hair and make-up, sparkles and classic autumnal hues. Many of those in the crowd at the waterfront venue donned WORLD themselves.
Wellington Fashion Week has a dual trade and consumer focus, with members of the public able to buy tickets to the shows. Designers will be sending their new spring/summer 2013-14 collections down the catwalk, as well as their autumn/winter 2013 collections. WORLD, along with other high-profile local designers, didn't show at last year's New Zealand Fashion Week in Auckland - the only annual international fashion event in New Zealand.
Earlier this year, the organisers of New Zealand Fashion Week announced changes to the event in 2013, including a shorter programme of trade shows and a greater focus on consumers. Wellington Fashion Week runs until April 7.
High winds might have seen most Wellingtonians pull on their polar fleeces yesterday, but it was high glamour as the capital's second Fashion Week kicked off last night.Fashion Week organiser Cameron Sneddon said he hoped the event, which concludes on Sunday, would boost the New Zealand fashion industry.
Auckland-based designers World showed their current autumn/winter collection, 'Boring Gets You Nowhere', to a sold-out crowd at Odlins Plaza on the waterfront. Richly textured fabrics - lace, velvet, sequins, gold-flecked tweed and rabbit fur accents - gave an edge to the label's elegant tailoring.
Opening night at WFW kicked off with a bang, literally, as high winds shook the marquee, but did nothing to dampen the people's spirits inside it. Viewers flocked to experience the first night of WFW, with a lot of different looks and trends coming through in their very own outfit selection. Strong colour blocking was a key theme shown, with oranges and reds being worn by those wanting to stand out from the crowd.
Black was the staple colour of the night, but many spiced it up by layering different textures and styles, addling a little something extra to that basic wardrobe classic. For male attendees, the bow-tie and suits combo was a great look, also staying in trend with what WORLD had to showcase later on in the night. Leather and sequins where also a big hit, with some fantastic jackets and dresses incorporating these textures being shown off.
The nights most favoured ensemble was a piece later displayed on the catwalk, with a handful of males attending the show all spotted wearing the newly designed floral suit, created by none other than the fabulous WORLD team themselves.
