Seafood Dinner Recipes

Move over, chicken, there's a new protein in town! Salmon, tuna, scallops, shrimp — a sea of dinnertime possibilities await once you leave dry land. Plus, a diet which includes oily fish, with their prevalence of omega 3 polyunsaturated fatty acids, is known to help reduce the risk of heart disease. New research from Harvard School of Public Health has examined the direct link between levels of the fatty acids in the blood and death rates, and claims eating these types of fish in old age could contribute to extending lifespan by more than two years.
Large-scale commercial fishing practices have devastated stocks of many fish species, but check for Marine Conservation Society-approved suppliers and support small-scale fishermen to source some responsibly caught specimens so you can feel good about enjoying some of these healthy and delicious recipes. We've rounded up only the most down-right delicious seafood recipes possible. One bite and the family will dive right in!

Easy Halibut Fillets with Herb Butter

       

Total Time(Prep+Cook): 11 mins

(3+8)

Serves: 2



Ingredients:

  • 2 halibut fillets
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped parsley (or other herb of your choice) or 2 tablespoons finely chopped tarragon (or other herb of your choice) or 2 tablespoons finely chopped dill (or other herb of your choice)
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil

Directions:

  1. Make little aluminum foil trays sized to fit each fillet.
  2. Wipe the bottom of each tray with olive oil.
  3. Put the fillets ugly side up (skin side even if the skin is now removed) on the trays.
  4. Mix the butter with the chopped herbs throughly.
  5. Place 1 tablespoon of the butter on the halibut.
  6. Broil for 4 minutes.
  7. Carefully turn each fillet over, not spilling any of the melted herb butter.
  8. Broil for 4 more minutes.
  9. Remove and plate, pouring the melted sauce over each fillet.

Mussels in White Wine and Garlic

       

Total Time(Prep+Cook): 25 mins

(10+15)

Serves: 4



Ingredients:

  • 4 lbs live mussels
  • 2 cups dry white wine
  • 4 large shallots, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup mixed fresh herbs, such as flat-leaf parsley,chervil,or basil,chopped
  • 6 tablespoons butter, cut into pieces

Directions:

  1. Rinse and scrub mussels under cold water.
  2. Using your fingers or pairing knife, remove beards (strings that hang from the mussel shells), and discard.
  3. In a large stockpot set over medium heat, combine wine, shallots, garlic, and salt.
  4. Simmer 5 minutes.
  5. Add mussels, cover and increase heat to High.
  6. Cook until all mussles are open, about 5 minutes.
  7. Stir in herbs and butter.
  8. Remove from heat.
  9. Divide mussels and broth among four bowls.
  10. Serve immediately.
