The Subtle Art of Drinking and Appreciating Good Wine

Every wine critique and connoisseur out there has their own rules for how to drink wine. Out of all these different suggestions there are a few that stand out and seem to be the same for anyone that knows anything about wines. The most common rule that every connoisseur is sure of is to have wine with food: red for meat and white with poultry and seafood. That is the general rule, however there are exceptions based on how much flavor is in the food. Many different websites offer tips and suggestions based on what you are cooking to help.
Now that you have selected the proper wine, you need to serve the it at the right temperature. White wine should be served at a temperature of about 53 degrees and red should be served at about 63 degrees. There are many wine fridges that can be bought to keep wine at these temperatures or you can just estimate. Drinking wine at these recommended keeps them the most flavorful that they can possibly be.
When trying to keep wine at a chilled temperature while drinking it, make sure to place it in a glass with a long enough stem so that the heat from your hand holding the glass does not heat up the wine. When you find the perfect wine glass, make sure to pour your wine slowing and let the glass breathe for a little while before drinking it. This allows the best and more complex flavors to come through.
Now if you have a much older bottle, you will have to decant it before you can drink it. This is a very simple process. When you are pouring the wine into your glass just make sure to pour it through something that will strain off any sediment that has formed in the wine over the many years. Usually you can just pour it through a cheese cloth or even a coffee filter. Also the wine will have to breathe for longer than a young bottle of wine a few hours at most.
Time to drink your wine. Before you take that first sip make sure to take a big inhale of the wine itself. Much of what we taste comes from what we smell, so make sure to take it all in before you take the first sip. When you do take that first sip, hold it in your mouth for a few seconds before swallowing it, savor it. The first sip of wine should allow you to taste all the flavors and really enjoy what you are drinking. Wine is not for rushing through, it is meant to be savored, enjoyed and sipped. Everyone has their own favorite wines and everyone needs to figure out what they are for themselves.
