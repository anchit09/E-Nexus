Designer Spotlight: Nicolino

Accessible, laid-back luxury. To most people, these words would appear to be a contradiction in terms. But to designer Nicki Cozzolino, they represent the perfect sartorial synthesis, as evidenced by her brand new label, Nicolino.
The New York-based designer grew up in suburban New Jersey and studied fashion design at Parsons The New School for Design after completing a degree in fine art and psychology at George Washington University. She cut her teeth working on Richard Chai's namesake collection and helped launch his contemporary diffusion line, Richard Chai LOVE, before striking out on her own for Fall 2013.
"The core philosophy of the brand is simple," said Cozzolino. "[To] craft luxurious, thoughtful yet accessible styles that speak to a range of women, taking them through their days, nights and all the other happenings in-between."
In practical terms, this translates to leather-trimmed tunics and dresses, sexy harem pants (another non-oxymoron), distressed leather jackets, slinky suits, and beautifully cut outerwear. Sheer silk blouses add a dash of femininity to tailored trousers; fitted minis are balanced by boxy tops; while a classic black knit sweater boasts a playful embroidered illustration of a woman in an exaggerated bowler hat. And all of the collection's prints are based on photos taken by the designer herself while on vacation in St. Maarten.
"Having grown up such an observer, studying people, I'm cognizant of how much the human psyche plays into the art of dressing and I really enjoy designing from that perspective," noted Cozzolino of her psychological-meets-creative approach.
Think of it as Fashion 101.
Nicolino retails from $225 to $1,495; the Fall 2013 collection will be carried at specialty boutiques nationwide, including Elements in Dallas, Satine in Los Angeles and online at Nicolino.
